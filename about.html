About Me

Hi my name is Jessie Paulson and currently I am a senior at UW-Madison pursuing degrees in Environmental Studies, Cartography and GIS, and Political Science. I am well versed in ArcGIS, ENVI, and Adobe products through my cartography and remote sensing courses as well my GIS lab work. This semester, I am using my cartographic skills to further learn how to create terrain maps, tile design maps, web map applications, and other infographic maps. I also have experience in ENVI and using aerial imagery as I completed a research project analyzing aerial photography for Hanoi, Vietnam in my remote sensing course this past Spring. The project focused on Hanoiâ€™s urban transformation, the resulting land use changes, and the potentially necessary policy changes needed for this type of urban growth. Furthermore, I have learned and practiced many mapping methods used in GIS and spatial analysis in my GIS focused courses.

In addition to my academic preparations and computer skills, I believe my internship positions and extra-curricular involvement contribute to the strength of my cartographic skillset. This past summer, I was the Research and GIS Intern at Washington DC Economic Partnership (WDCEP). At WDCEP, I created interactive ESRI maps including a map of over 50 different historical properties within commercial and industrial zones of the District that either could be or have been repurposed for new uses. Also, currently, I am the aerial imagery intern at the SILVIS Lab in the Department of Forestry and Wildlife at UW-Madison. My team and I are in charge of finding and digitizing historic imagery of fires that burned at least 50 structures occurring between 1970 and 1999. Finally, I am the current president for the Geography Club at UW-Madison and am serving my second year on its leadership team. Many of these projects referenced you can find on my website. Feel free to contact me with any question and/or comments. Thanks for stopping by!
